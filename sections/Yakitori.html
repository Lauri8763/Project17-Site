Yakitori

Grilled chicken in Japanese style

Yakitori are grilled chicken skewers made from bite sized pieces of meat from all different parts of the chicken, such as the breasts, thighs, skin, liver and other innards. Usually made to order and cooked over charcoal, yakitori is a popular, inexpensive dish commonly enjoyed together with a glass of beer.

Receipt:
  • Chicken fillet - 500 gr
  • Soy sauce -3 tbsp
  • Honey - 1 tbsp
  • Garlic - 1 clove
  • Ginger root - 10 gr
  • Sunflower oil - 2 tbsp
  • Sesame - 1 tsp

    • Wash the chicken fillet, dry it with paper towel and cut it into long strips. Mix soy sauce, honey, finely chopped garlic and ginger. Place the pieces of fillet in the marinade and leave for 1 hour. String the chicken fillets on skewers in waves. Heat a grill pan, pour in sunflower oil and place the skewers. Fry on high heat for 1 minute on four sides. Place Yakitori on a plate, sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve. Bon appetit!
