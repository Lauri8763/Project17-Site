Tsukune

Minced chicken patties in Japanese cuisine

Tsukune is a Japanese chicken meatball most often cooked yakitori style and sometimes covered in a sweet soy or yakitori tare. The ground meat (or minced meat) in Japan could be chicken, pork, or even fish. They beat or ground finely the meat and then kneaded and moulded into balls or sticks using a binder such as chicken egg, potato starch, and yam, and flavours such as ginger, salt, and soy sauce.

Receipt:
  • Minced chicken - 400 gr
  • Onion - 0.5 pc
  • Egg yolk - 1 pc
  • Ginger - 0.5cm - 1 tsp grated
  • Salt - 0.25 tsp
  • Potato starch - 4 tbsp
  • Vegetable oil - 2 tsp

    • Very finely chop the onion. Place the minced chicken, onion, egg yolk, grated ginger, salt and potato starch in a large bowl. Mix well until very sticky. Divide into 15 pieces and shape into small round or oval cutlets. If you are cooking tsukune on coals, soak bamboo skewers in water 30-40 minutes before cooking. Put 3 cutlets on each skewer, fry them over hot coals until they are ready, 2-3 minutes on each side. If cooking in a frying pan, then heat the oil in a frying pan over a medium heat, fry the cutlets for 3-4 minutes on each side until soft. Pour the sauce into the frying pan and quickly boil it over a high heat, turning the cutlets over and over so that they are all coated in the sauce. Bon appetit!
