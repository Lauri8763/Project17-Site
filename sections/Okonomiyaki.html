Okonomiyaki

Eastern savory pancakes

Okonomiyaki is a delicious savory Japanese treat served in a pancake shape. This dish includes a variety of toppings with sweet and creamy sauces. Its name translates to what you want grilled, which is appropriate as you can choose your toppings whenever ordering okonomiyaki. The typical okonomiyaki ingredients include a batter and shredded cabbage, though the remaining ingredients vary greatly and include options like octopus, kimchi, pork and et cetera.

Receipt:
  • Tempura mix - 30 gr
  • Dashi powder - 1 tsp
  • Wheat flour - 160 gr
  • Salt - 0.25 tsp
  • Sugar - 0.25 tsp
  • White cabbage - 480 gr
  • Green onion - 40 gr
  • Pork belly - 160 gr
  • Pickled ginger - 10 gr
  • Okonomiyaki sauce - to your taste
  • Japanese mayonnaise - to your taste
  • Tuna flakes - to your taste
  • Chicken egg - 4 units
  • Water - 240 ml
  • Vegetable oil - 520 ml

    • To make the tenkasa (Crispy bits of deep-fried flour dough used in Japanese cooking), mix the tempura flour with 40 millilitres of cold water until smooth. Heat the vegetable oil in a small saucepan and pour the tempura into it in small drops. Cook for a minute or two, then scoop out the fried batter and place on paper towels to get rid of any excess fat. Dilute the dashi powder in 200 millilitres of water. Mix the flour, salt and sugar in a large bowl, pour in the dashi broth, add the eggs and knead the dough. Add finely chopped cabbage, spring onions, chopped pickled ginger and the tenkasu balls from step 1 to the dough. Mix well. Heat a frying pan well, place a quarter of the prepared mixture in it, shape the mixture into a round pancake about 2 cm thick. Place pork slices on the pancake and fry over a medium heat until the okonomiyaki thickens. Then flip the okonomiyaki gently with a wide spatula and cover the pan with a lid. Cook for another 5-7 minutes. Brush the finished okonomiyaki with okonomiyaki sauce, mash with Japanese mayonnaise and sprinkle the tuna shavings over the top. Bon appetit!
