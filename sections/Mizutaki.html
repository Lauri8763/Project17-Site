Mizutaki

Chicken stew with Japanese features

Mizutaki, literally meaning ‘cooked in the water’ is a traditional food in Fukuoka prefecture, Japan, and is typically known as a Japanese chicken hot pot dish. Mizutaki is a cooking method in which ingredients such as chopped chicken with bones and vegetables are slowly boiled in hot water. Unlike any other soups, the seasonings in mizutaki such as soy sauce and salt are not added even when the soup stock has been made in advance.

Receipt:
  • Chicken fillet - 1 kg
  • Chicken broth - 200 ml water + pinch of salt + 600 gr chicken bones
  • Chicken liver - 50 gr
  • Chicken stomachs - 50g
  • Tofu - 50 gr
  • Chrysanthemum leaves - 0.5 part
  • Shiitake - 4 pieces
  • Chinese cabbage - 0.25 part
  • Cauliflower - 150g
  • Ponzu sauce - 10 ml
  • Leeks - 20 gr
  • Momiji oroshi - 2 red peppers + 100 gr radish
  • Rice cake - 4 pcs

    • Rinse the chicken bones in hot water in a saucepan. Add cold water to the pot and cook over a high heat (when the foam appears, remove it). Cook until the liquid has reduced to 1/3. Remove the bones from the pot, grind with a pestle and put back into the boiling water. When ready, strain the stock. Cut the chicken fillets into 50g pieces and place in a deep pan. Pour water into the pan so that it covers the chicken. Braise over low heat for 25 minutes, skimming off the foam whenever it appears. Turn off the heat and leave the pan on a hot cooker for 30 minutes. Chop the stomachs and liver. Slice the tofu and place on a plate. Also put chopped chrysanthemum leaves, mushrooms, cabbage, leeks and Momiji oroshi on the same plate. Put the chicken fillets, liver and stomachs in a clay pot. Pour the stock and add ponzu sauce, then put the tofu, vegetables and mushrooms. You eat, and the dish cooks over low heat the whole time. Towards the end, when all is eaten, you can boil the noodles and eat with rice cakes. Bon appetit!
